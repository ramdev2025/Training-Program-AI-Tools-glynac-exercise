
*** HEALTHCARE COMPLIANCE POLICY DOCUMENT (SAMPLE) ***

SECTION 1: HIPAA COMPLIANCE & PRIVACY POLICIES
1.1 Uses and Disclosures of Protected Health Information (PHI)
Our organization may use or disclose PHI for treatment, payment, and health care operations without patient authorization. All other uses require written authorization. We maintain a log of disclosures for six years.
1.2 Patient Rights
Patients have the right to inspect and copy their PHI, request amendments, and receive an accounting of disclosures.
1.3 Security Safeguards
Physical access to servers is restricted. Electronic access requires unique user IDs and passwords. Data is encrypted at rest but currently not encrypted in transit within the internal network.

SECTION 2: EMPLOYMENT PRACTICES & EQUAL OPPORTUNITY
2.1 Equal Employment Opportunity
We are an equal opportunity employer. We do not discriminate based on race, color, religion, sex, or national origin.
2.2 Hiring Process
All candidates must pass a background check. Medical examinations are required for all applicants prior to a job offer being made.
2.3 Reasonable Accommodations
Employees requesting accommodations for disabilities must submit a formal request to HR. Requests are reviewed on a case-by-case basis.

SECTION 3: DATA PROTECTION & STATE PRIVACY
3.1 Data Retention
Patient records are retained for 10 years.
3.2 Breach Notification
In the event of a data breach, we will notify affected individuals within 90 days of discovery.

SECTION 4: VENDOR AGREEMENTS (STANDARD TERMS)
4.1 Business Associate Agreements (BAA)
All vendors handling PHI must sign a BAA.
4.2 Liability
Vendor liability for data breaches is limited to the value of the contract.
4.3 Audits
We reserve the right to audit vendor security practices once every two years.

*** END OF DOCUMENT ***
